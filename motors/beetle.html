People's Beetle

In this article, I'll explore one of my family's car restoration projects: a VW Beetle. Although it has many other nicknames commonly used in different countries, for example Käfer in Germany (its home country), or Carocha in Portugal, it was officially designated Volkswagen Type 1.

Family Background

A long time ago, even before this car appeared in my family, it was my father, Inácio, who took the first steps in this tough road.

Born in the Portuguese countryside, near Serra da Estrela, soon moved to the capital city, Lisbon, when with only 16 years old was looking for better life opportunities. There he started working in the garages of the national postal company (CTT), in the area of vehicles repair and maintenance, which became his life's profession until today.

After almost a decade, with only the one and a half year exit to fulfill his military obligation, decided to emigrate to Canada with his brother. With this new reality, he continued to develop his craftship in the auto area, doing all the process from panel beater to paint job.

A few years later, he met my mother and after a while, they got married. My brother, Nelson, was born in 1985 and within five years, well, it was my turn. The comeback to Portugal was always in their minds and in 1993, we all settled down in a village in the center region of Portugal.

In 2001, after a few years working for others, my father had finally reunited the conditions to start his own business and build his autoshop.

The passion for cars, particularly old ones, was already passed to the young generation, when my brother decided to left school and do a car painting specialization course.

Our bonds got stronger by absorving and sharing the passion and legacy of the old man. We believe in family projects.

Steven
