People's Beetle

In this article, I'll explore one of my family's car restoration projects: a VW Beetle. Although it has many other nicknames commonly used in different countries, for example Käfer in Germany (its home country), or Carocha in Portugal, it was officially designated Volkswagen Type 1.

Family Background

A long time ago, even before this car joined my family, it was my father, Inácio, who took the first steps in this bumpy road.

Born in the Portuguese countryside, near Serra da Estrela, soon he moved to the capital city, Lisbon, at a very young age (16 years old) looking for better life opportunities. There he started working at the workshops of the national postal company (CTT), in vehicles repair and maintenance, which became his profession until today.

After almost a decade, and fulfilling his military obligation for one and a half year within this period, he decided to emigrate to Canada with his brother. In this new reality, he continued to develop his craftsmanship, doing all the process from panel beater to paint job.

A few years later, he met my mother and after a while, they got married. My brother, Nelson, was born in 1985 and after five years, well, it was my turn. The comeback to Portugal was always in their minds and, in 1993, we all settled down in a village in the center region of Portugal.

In 2001, after a few years working for someone else, my father had finally reunited the conditions to start his own business and build his autoshop.

The passion for cars, particularly old ones, had already been passed to the young generation and my brother just confirmed it, when he decided to leave school and do a car painting specialization course.

Beetle's Journey

On a sunny Sunday in 2004, and after a brief research in the advertisement's section of the regional newspaper Ocasião, which by that time was almost the only way to buy and sell cars in the country, me and my dad decided to embark on a small trip to Tomar. There we found this old and rusty car parked in a barn. After a quick look, and since our idea had always been to do a full restoration, the main requirement for us wasn't to find a car in great conditions, but an interesting price instead. Therefore, we made a deal for €600 and on that day we sent it to our autoshop.

The first steps were, obviously, the entire disassembly of the car and, during that process, we discovered a lot of holes and rotten parts resulted from years of abandonment. One of the characteristics of these types of cars is the easy separation between the chassis and the upper body, which allowed us to do a better job by reaching and repairing some hidden parts. The engine was also dismantled, part by part, and upgraded from 1300cc to 1600cc. This specific work was done by some mechanic friends, who accompanied us in this multiyear journey. For their contribution and all the others who helped us keeping the motivation and focus on the job, we are very thankful.

As some parts were in an awful state, we had to purchase several pieces. From fenders, headlights, door sills, to small pieces of the braking system, steering wheel, etc.

Working only on the short free time available during almost 5 years, we had finally ended the first part of the restoration. Some time later, after painting and putting back everything in the car, we discovered an anomaly in the paint job. This was caused by the use of an anticorrosive product, which had been applied in the early stages of the bare metal treatment. And suddenly, we were in the middle of an unexpected thunderstorm.

The project was suspended in the following years, not only because our motivation had been hit hard, but also those times of economic recession in Portugal didn't help at all. Meanwhile, our priorities had also changed.

The last chapter of the restoration was written over the last 2 years. We took a few steps back in the paint job, scratched almost the whole car and, especially, where that product was causing problems. Then, we treated it the best way possible. The final round took place in the first quarantine period of COVID-19, when we had more time to reach the end of this long road.

In the end, our bonds just got stronger by absorbing and sharing the passion and legacy of the old man. We believe in family projects.

Steven
