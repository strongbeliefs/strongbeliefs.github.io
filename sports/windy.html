Windy Days

According to ancient Greek philosophy, air is one of the four classical elements along with water, earth and fire. Wind, as the flow of air, is probably the most underrated by us, Humans. However, when some people see it as a weakness, others may see it as a strength. And that's exactly the case of our first guest and new friend, Julia, who is a polish sailor, coach and judge.

Set Sail From Krakow

Julia was born in Krakow (Kraków), one of the oldest cities of Poland (Polska), in 1997. She recalls, when she was an 8-year-old little girl, being by the Vistula (Wisła) river and seeing some boats, while her father, Marcin, asked her if she would like to try a new sport, sailing. Despite looking back and describing herself as a very busy kid with many additional activities, from piano and theater classes to swimming and English lessons, at that moment she just didn't feel like trying and that's why she said no to her father's suggestion. Her twin siblings, Klara and Kuba, who also became sailors at a very young age, were born this year, 2005.

After one year, however, when Julia was 9 years old, her father decided to have a different approach. He just signed me up. He didn't even ask, actually! That's my dad's ‘style’. He just told me. I don't remember exactly now, but it was something also bugging me, like I felt I could try.... Back then, she already appreciated her parents' educational purpose. They always wanted to really educate us in many ways [...] and were always looking for different activities for us. And she's thankful for that.

She started learning how to sail in a course for kids on vacation, during the summer of 2006. In this sailing club based in the Bagry lake, she met her first official coach, Grzegorz. Although Julia and her siblings were being helped by their father in the very beginning, because he had some sailing experience, they knew that Mr. (Pan) Grzegorz is the greatest one for the basics. Even if she and her coach only met in the clubs for the trainings and regattas, an enormous admiration and respect for Pan Grzegorz grew over time.

“He is absolutely marvelous! He is super patient with kids, super calm. I never saw him angry, never! Never shouting! I mean, coaches shout due to the wind, the water... but he never shouted at me or any other kid, because of anger, never! He is, seriously, the greatest one.”
